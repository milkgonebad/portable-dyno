Who drives the car for the test?

A factory trained dyno operator drives the car onto the dyno and runs the test.

What does it measure?

The Dyno can measure the following: Horsepower, torque, air/fuel ratio, boost, air intake temperature, fully integrated weather station measuring humidity, air temperature and isobaric pressure.

Why do a dyno run?

To establish a baseline for your car or truck. Find out how much you've gained by adding that new air intake, headers, ECU flash or fuel injection. You can also see how your air/fuel ratio compares to your horsepower torque to make sure you've tuned your car to its optimal efficiency.

How much does it cost?

No two events or appointments are the same so "its best to email" or call for an accurate quote.

Can I make adjustments to see impact?

Adjustments should be done with a scheduled tuning appointment, that way we can focus on just your car.

Will you make adjustments to see impact?

Only when scheduled for a tuning appointment.

Can it damage my engine?

The eddy current steady state dyno simulates road like conditions. If your car is in good working order then it should be no different than taking a spirited drive through the countryside.

Do you redline the engine?

We ask the customer how far they'd like us to run up the RPMs. Typically we run it up until it stops producing horsepower, which is about 1500 RPM below redline for most.

Who is responsible if something breaks during the test?

The dyno operator is not responsible for any damage to the automobile caused due to engine or drive train failure. Remember the dyno simulates real world conditions with the vehicle accelerating to measure its performance. You can specify a max RPM before the run begins.

I just installed a new 800hp engine, can you help break it in for me?

Absolutely! The steady state eddy current dyno can hold a specific speed, load and ramp rate to maximize the ideal break in conditions for your specific motor and drive line.

I have a large car collection, could you come to my facility and run each one?

Absolutely! We first warm up each car to optimal running temperature and then run a series of simulated driving conditions. This ensures the car gets all fluids up to temp and burns off any moisture in the engine and exhaust system. Running your car on the dyno ensures no chipped paint, no risking accidents while driving on the road and minimal mileage on the car. Upon request we also inspect all systems and report any findings to your mechanic so when you take out the car it's 100% ready to go.

Can you do a dyno day for my club?

Club events generally have a ten vehicle minimum. All expectations and logistics should be scheduled at least two weeks in advance.

Can you set up the dyno anywhere?

The dyno requires a hard stable level surface to set up. Concrete and asphalt work best, but a one inch 4x12' steel plate or plywood will work in a pinch.

What does the dyno need for power?

A 220V 60Htz power source should be available within 150' to 200'. Generators can be used if they produce an inverted 6000 Watt or higher pure sinewave powersource. Email or call to confirm your site is suitable.
